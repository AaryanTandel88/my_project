Core Concept: Mindful eating means paying full attention to the experience of eating and drinking, both inside and outside the body.

Practice Mindful Eating

  • Eat slowly: Take time to chew thoroughly and savor each bite.
  • Eliminate distractions: Turn off TV and put away phones during meals.
  • Listen to hunger cues: Eat when you're hungry, stop when you're satisfied (not stuffed).
  • Appreciate your food: Consider where your food came from and the effort that went into preparing it.
  • Use all senses: Notice colors, smells, textures, and flavors of your food.
  • Avoid emotional eating: Find other ways to cope with stress, boredom, or sadness.
  • Don't skip meals: Regular meals help prevent extreme hunger and overeating.
  • Enjoy your food: Allow yourself to enjoy treats in moderation without guilt.

Benefits of Mindful Eating

  • Better digestion
  • Improved relationship with food
  • Greater satisfaction with smaller portions
  • Reduced stress around eating
  • More enjoyment of meals
